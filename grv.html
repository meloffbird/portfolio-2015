Celebrating the music room experience at James B. Hunt Library

Amplifying Hunt

Scroll Down
Date
May 2015
Project Details
UI + UX + User Research + Exhibition
Team
Myself + Maddie Bone

[1] User Research

The media and music production rooms in James B. Hunt offer both graduate and undergraduate students the opportunity to produce audio work with industry-level production equipment and software. Unfortunately the process by which students are required to reserve, access and use these spaces is unnecessarily cumbersome. To better understand the limitations of the current system I interviewed student users , all with varying levels of experience in music production.

It soon became clear how uncomfortable and unsure the students were about the “rules” of the music rooms. First time users thought they were reserved for graduate students while frequent users often forgot where to make reservations and how to request the doors to be unlocked. From these conversations I was able to better understand the problem.

Key Problem & Resulting Objectives

The Problem: The existing reservation process required an understanding of the libraries security procedures, as well as multiple interactions with online and in-person resources. Only frequent users were able to succesfully complete the process without hitting knowledge or comfort barriers, while new users struggled and often gave up middway through.

Design Objective: Encourage engagement by streamlining the reservation process from start to finish.

[2] Touch to Start

The New Reservation App

To remove as many unnecessary steps as possible, the new reservation system would began on iPads mounted in the wall next to each of the doors which would allow users to claim existing reservations, as well as make new ones, without having to go to the Ask Us desk two floors below.

Once the users has confirmed their reservation a Hunt employee will be notified to come to the room in order to unlock the door. Once at the room, the employee can swipe their University ID in order to release the iPad from the mount, and transfer it to the standing mount located within the room.
